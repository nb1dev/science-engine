Understanding your report

Key terms explained

📖 Glossary — key terms used in this report
Butyrate
A short-chain fatty acid (SCFA) produced when bacteria ferment fiber. It's the primary fuel for your gut lining cells and has anti-inflammatory properties.
SCFA / Short-Chain Fatty Acids
Beneficial compounds produced by bacteria fermenting fiber. Includes butyrate, acetate, and propionate. These nourish your gut, regulate immunity, and influence mood.
Shannon Diversity
A scientific measure of species diversity. A higher score means more variety of different bacterial species — a more resilient, adaptable gut ecosystem.
Guild
A group of bacteria that share a similar ecological function — like a specialist team. There are six guilds in this report, each with a different job in your gut.
Dysbiosis
An imbalance in gut bacteria — when harmful species overgrow or beneficial species are depleted. The opposite of a healthy, balanced microbiome.
Prebiotic
Food for your beneficial bacteria. Prebiotics are fibers and compounds that your own body can't digest, but your bacteria can — feeding and supporting their growth.
Probiotic
Live beneficial bacteria in supplement form. Taken orally, they travel to your gut and can colonise, compete with less beneficial bacteria, and produce helpful compounds.
Gut-Brain Axis
The two-way communication network between your gut and your brain, primarily via the vagus nerve. Your gut bacteria influence this signalling — affecting mood, stress, and cognitive function.
